ABOUT US

Cardiovascular diseases or Congestive Heart Failure is one of the leading causes of deaths all over the world. It accounts for almost a million peopleâ€™s deaths on a yearly basis, contributing 31% of the deaths worldwide. Heart diseases or cardiovascular diseases refer to the condition which directly or indirectly affect the functionality of the heart. Conditions involving blocked blood vessels which may lead to heart attack and other strokes also come under heart diseases. Detection of heart failure in a patient at early stages is necessary because the condition of the heart worsens day by day if not given the right treatment. Expectation of the life increases only when the patient receives right treatment at right stage and right level of severity of the disease.


People with cardiovascular disease, who are at high cardiovascular risk (due to the presence of one or more risk factors such as hypertension, diabetes, hyperlipidemia or already established disease) need early detection and management. Considering the current situation worldwide, i.e., global pandemic and lockdowns, one might be very skeptical to go to hospitals or visit doctors. Getting an appointment of a cardiologist would also be difficult during these times. Even for the doctors it is difficult to meet their patients on the regular basis and check their health condition.


To overcome this problem, we have come up with a solution of creating a website using a machine learning models, that will help such patients to find out if there is any presence of heart disease based on the 13 medical parameters such as age, sex, blood pressure, cholesterol, Electrocardiography, Diabetes etc., obtained from the tests which they input and if the analysis shows the presence of the disease, the website will allow them find cardiologists and contact them online to share their reports. Doctors will be given access to all the reports and patients heart disease analysis history (All the analysis done by the patient will be stored in the history tab) so that they can carefully examine it and suggest further steps. This would be one point of contact for both patients and doctors.
